# Books on Modeling and Simulation of Crystal Growth
*Published after 1990 — compiled June 2026*

---

## 1. Foundational & General Texts

**Markov, I. V.**
*Crystal Growth for Beginners: Fundamentals of Nucleation, Crystal Growth and Epitaxy*
World Scientific, 1995 (1st ed.); 2003 (2nd ed.); 2016 (3rd ed.)
Covers nucleation theory, surface kinetics, epitaxial growth mechanisms, and Monte Carlo modelling of surface processes. Widely used as a graduate-level introduction.

---

**Pimpinelli, A. & Villain, J.**
*Physics of Crystal Growth*
Cambridge University Press, 1998
Statistical physics treatment of crystal surface dynamics, step flow, instabilities, and kinetic Monte Carlo simulations of surface growth. A standard reference for thin-film and epitaxial modelling.

---

**Barabási, A.-L. & Stanley, H. E.**
*Fractal Concepts in Surface Growth*
Cambridge University Press, 1995
Introduces stochastic growth models (KPZ equation and relatives), scaling laws, and simulation approaches for surface morphology evolution during deposition and crystal growth.

---

## 2. Solidification & Melt Growth

**Kurz, W. & Fisher, D. J.**
*Fundamentals of Solidification*
Trans Tech Publications, 4th ed., 1998 (earlier editions 1984, 1989, 1992)
The standard reference for dendritic growth theory, solute redistribution, and solidification microstructure models; extensively cited in crystal growth simulation literature.

---

**Dantzig, J. A. & Rappaz, M.**
*Solidification*
EPFL Press, 2009; revised and expanded 2nd ed., 2016
Comprehensive graduate textbook covering thermodynamic and transport models of solidification, phase-field methods, and computational approaches to microstructure prediction. Widely regarded as the definitive modern treatment.

---

**Glicksman, M. E.**
*Principles of Solidification: An Introduction to Modern Casting and Crystal Growth Concepts*
Springer, 2011
Covers thermodynamic and kinetic theories of liquid-solid transformation, solute redistribution, and modelling of bulk crystal growth including Czochralski and Bridgman configurations.

---

## 3. Phase-Field and Continuum Modelling

**Provatas, N. & Elder, K. R.**
*Phase-Field Methods in Materials Science and Engineering*
Wiley-VCH, 2010
Systematic development of phase-field models for solidification, grain growth, and crystal growth; includes numerical implementation details and benchmark cases.

---

## 4. Bulk Crystal Growth from Melt — Simulation-Focused

**Scheel, H. J. & Fukuda, T. (Eds.)**
*Crystal Growth Technology*
John Wiley & Sons, 2003
Edited volume with dedicated chapters on computational simulation (Czochralski, Bridgman, floating-zone), heat and mass transport modelling, and process design; contributions from Derby, Yeckel, Kakimoto, Polezhaev, and others.

---

**Müller, G., Métois, J. J. & Rudolph, P. (Eds.)**
*Crystal Growth — From Fundamentals to Technology*
Elsevier, 2004
Proceedings-based monograph containing authoritative survey chapters on numerical modelling of bulk crystal growth from melt and solution (Derby & Yeckel), convection control, defect modelling, and multi-scale simulation strategies.

---

**Wu, L.**
*Modeling and Simulation of Czochralski Bulk Crystal Growth Process: Investigation of Transport Effects in Melt and Gas Phases*
VDM Verlag, 2009
Focused monograph on finite-element Navier–Stokes simulation of the Czochralski process, covering turbulence modelling, mesh generation, and oxygen transport in silicon melts.

---

## 5. Handbooks with Major Simulation Content

**Hurle, D. T. J. (Ed.)**
*Handbook of Crystal Growth*, Vol. 1 (Fundamentals: Parts A & B), Vol. 2 (Bulk Crystal Growth), Vol. 3 (Thin Films and Epitaxy)
North-Holland / Elsevier, 1993–1994
Three-volume reference covering thermodynamics, transport phenomena, morphological stability, dendritic growth theory, numerical simulation methods, and defect dynamics. The benchmark reference of the 1990s.

---

**Dhanaraj, G., Byrappa, K., Prasad, V. & Dudley, M. (Eds.)**
*Springer Handbook of Crystal Growth*
Springer, 2010
Comprehensive single-volume handbook spanning melt, solution, and vapour growth. Dedicated sections cover computational fluid dynamics (CFD) for melt flows, heat and mass transfer simulation, defect modelling, and process design for Czochralski and Bridgman systems.

---

**Nishinaga, T. & Rudolph, P. (Eds.)**
*Handbook of Crystal Growth*, 2nd ed., Vols. I–III
Elsevier, 2014–2015
Updated successor to Hurle's handbook. Volume II (Bulk Crystal Growth) contains extensive simulation chapters on Czochralski silicon (Friedrich, von Ammon & Müller), floating-zone silicon (Muiznieks et al.), fluid dynamics and modelling (Kakimoto & Gao), segregation, and defect dynamics. Vol. I covers phase-field and kinetic Monte Carlo methods for fundamental growth processes.

---

## 6. Epitaxial Growth and Thin-Film Simulation

**Michely, T. & Krug, J.**
*Islands, Mounds and Atoms: Patterns and Processes in Crystal Growth Far from Equilibrium*
Springer Series in Surface Sciences, 2004
Combines experimental STM observations with kinetic Monte Carlo and continuum models for epitaxial growth morphologies. Accessible bridge between atomic-scale simulation and mesoscale pattern formation.

---

**Kotrla, M., Papanicolaou, N. I., Vvedensky, D. D. & Wille, L. T. (Eds.)**
*Atomistic Aspects of Epitaxial Growth*
Kluwer Academic, 2002
NATO Science Series volume collecting contributions on kinetic Monte Carlo simulations, rate-equation models, and ab initio studies of epitaxial crystal growth.

---

## 7. Industrial Crystallization and Solution Growth

**Myerson, A. S. (Ed.)**
*Handbook of Industrial Crystallization*
Butterworth-Heinemann, 1993; 2nd ed. 2002; 3rd ed. (Cambridge) 2019
Covers nucleation, crystal growth kinetics, population balance models, and process simulation for industrial solution crystallization. Standard reference in pharmaceutical and chemical engineering contexts.

---

**Mullin, J. W.**
*Crystallization*
Butterworth-Heinemann, 4th ed., 2001
Authoritative text on crystal growth from solution including kinetic models, nucleation theory, and simulation of industrial crystallizers.

---

## 8. Vapour-Phase and CVD Growth

**Stringfellow, G. B.**
*Organometallic Vapor-Phase Epitaxy: Theory and Practice*
Academic Press, 2nd ed., 1999
Covers thermodynamic and kinetic modelling of OMVPE/MOCVD growth, reactor design, and computational simulation of gas-phase transport and surface reactions.

---

## 9. Defects and Point-Defect Dynamics

**Abe, T. & Shimura, F. (general context); Kulkarni, M. S. (chapter author in Nishinaga Vol. II)**
For point-defect dynamics and vacancy/interstitial modelling specific to Czochralski silicon, the primary book-length treatment is found in:

**Sinno, T., Dornberger, E., von Ammon, W., Brown, R. A. & Dupret, F.**
— Survey coverage in Nishinaga *Handbook of Crystal Growth* (2015) and in:

**Voronkov, V. V. & Falster, R.**
— Chapters in *Springer Handbook of Crystal Growth* (Dhanaraj et al., 2010), providing the mathematical models for grown-in defect control in silicon. For a dedicated monograph treatment see:

**Shimura, F.**
*Semiconductor Silicon Crystal Technology*
Academic Press, 1989; updated material in subsequent edited volumes through the 1990s–2000s.

---

## 10. Multi-Scale and Computational Materials Science

**Rapaport, D. C.**
*The Art of Molecular Dynamics Simulation*
Cambridge University Press, 1995; 2nd ed., 2004
Although general in scope, this is the standard reference for MD simulation methodology applicable to melt structure, nucleation, and near-interface dynamics in crystal growth modelling.

---

## Notes

- For journal-level simulation work (Karma, Rappaz, Derby, Beckermann, Steinbach), no single dedicated monograph yet replaces the handbook chapters and review articles, but the phase-field and solidification textbooks listed above (Provatas & Elder; Dantzig & Rappaz) provide the most complete book-length coverage.
- The Hurle (1993–94) and Nishinaga (2014–15) *Handbook of Crystal Growth* editions form the primary multi-volume reference spanning both phenomenology and simulation for all growth methods.
