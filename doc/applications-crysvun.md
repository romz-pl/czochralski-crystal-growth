# Bibliography of Peer-Reviewed Application Papers Using CrysVUn / CrysVUn++

This bibliography lists peer-reviewed journal articles in which the CrysVUn (also written CrysVUN, CrysVUN++, or, in its successor form, CrysMAS) software package was **used as a simulation tool** to model a crystal-growth or solidification process, as opposed to papers whose primary purpose is to describe the code itself. Entries are sorted chronologically by year of publication (and, within a year, alphabetically by first author).

> **Note on scope:** CrysVUn was developed at the Crystal Growth Laboratory (CGL) of the Fraunhofer Institute for Integrated Systems and Device Technology (IISB), Erlangen, Germany, by M. Kurz, A. Pusztai, G. Müller and co-workers, and was later commercialized/extended as CrysMAS. Papers describing the code architecture itself (e.g., Kurz, Pusztai & Müller 1999, "CrysVUN++, a Powerful Computer Code…") are intentionally excluded, per the request. Conference proceedings, book chapters, and non-peer-reviewed reports have also been excluded; only peer-reviewed journal articles are listed.

## Notes on completeness and verification

- Several entries above (35–38, 40, 41, 43) were identified through secondary citations, abstracts, and "Request PDF" summaries rather than through direct access to the full bibliographic record (volume/issue/page numbers). Author lists and exact citation details for these should be verified against the primary source (e.g., via Web of Science, Scopus, or the journal's own site) before use in a formal reference list, as several of them are Chinese-language *Journal of Crystal Growth*-adjacent or regional crystal-growth-society publications for which only English abstracts were retrievable.
- Entries 24 and 39 appear in both conference-proceedings and peer-reviewed-journal forms in different citation databases; only the peer-reviewed journal appearance is intended here, but this should be double-checked against the original source.
- Two seminal software-description papers were deliberately excluded per your request:

  - The related conference paper "Development of a new powerful computer code CrysVUN++ especially designed for fast simulation of bulk crystal growth processes."
- The historical review by J. Friedrich et al., "Erlangen — An Important Center of Crystal Growth and Epitaxy" (*Crystal Research and Technology*, 2020, 55, 1900053), while itself a review rather than an application paper, is a valuable secondary source cross-referencing many of the above CrysVUn application studies and is recommended as a starting point for further verification.
