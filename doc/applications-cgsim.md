# Bibliography of Peer-Reviewed Application Papers Using CGSim (STR Group)

This bibliography lists peer-reviewed journal articles that **applied** the CGSim (Crystal Growth Simulator) software package from STR Group Ltd. as a numerical modeling tool to study, design, or optimize crystal growth processes (Czochralski, LEC, VGF, Bridgman, directional solidification, TSSG, Kyropoulos, HEM, etc.). Papers that primarily *describe the code itself* (e.g., STR's own software-development or algorithm papers) are excluded, except where a methods-development paper also constitutes a substantive materials-application study. Entries are sorted chronologically by publication year.

*Note: this compilation was assembled from web and literature searches and, given the very large and dispersed literature spanning more than two decades and many material systems (Si, sapphire, GaAs, InP, GaSb, SiC, GaN, oxides, quartz, diamond), should be treated as a substantial but not guaranteed-complete listing. DOIs are provided where identified.*

## Notes on completeness and verification

- Several entries above (e.g., #2, #27, #28) have partial or reconstructed bibliographic details pulled from secondary citations, patents, or aggregator sites (ResearchGate, Science.gov, STR Group's own news/application pages) rather than being directly confirmed against the publisher's original record. These should be independently verified against the journal of record (DOI resolution) before citing.
- CGSim has also been used in a number of conference proceedings (ECS Transactions, MATEC Web of Conferences, IOP Conference Series) which are included only where they are peer-reviewed journal-adjacent series; pure conference abstracts without peer review were excluded.
- Additional application domains known to use CGSim but for which specific journal citations could not be fully confirmed in this search session include: AlN growth by PVT, HEM sapphire, quartz hydrothermal growth, and various oxide crystals (BGO, LiNbO3, YAG) — STR Group's own product pages reference in-house or co-authored studies in these areas that may warrant further targeted searching.
- This list should be treated as a strong starting point rather than a final, exhaustive bibliography; a systematic Scopus/Web of Science citation search on "CGSim" OR "Crystal Growth Simulator" AND "STR Group" would be needed to guarantee full coverage.
