# Peer-Reviewed Journal Articles on CrysVUn / CrysVUN++

Compiled from available bibliographic and citation data. Entries are peer-reviewed journal articles (conference proceedings volumes of *Journal of Crystal Growth* are included, as that journal publishes ICCG/IWMCG proceedings papers as regular peer-reviewed articles).

1. **Böttcher, K., Rudolph, P., Neubert, M., Kurz, M., Pusztai, A., Müller, G.** (1999). "Global temperature field simulation of the vapour pressure controlled Czochralski (VCZ) growth of 3″–4″ gallium arsenide crystals." *Journal of Crystal Growth*, 198/199, 349–354.

2. **Kurz, M., Pusztai, A., Müller, G.** (1999). "Development of a new powerful computer code CrysVUN++ especially designed for fast simulation of bulk crystal growth processes." *Journal of Crystal Growth*, 198/199, 101–106.

3. **Metzger, M., Kurz, M., Müller, G.** (1999). "Control of thermal conditions during crystal growth by inverse modeling." *Journal of Crystal Growth*, 203(1–2), 296–303.

4. **Backofen, R., Kurz, M., Müller, G.** (2000). "Process modeling of the industrial VGF growth process using the software package CrysVUN++." *Journal of Crystal Growth*, 211(1–4), 202–206.

5. **Friedrich, J., Dagner, J., Hainke, M., Müller, G.** (2003). "Numerical modeling of crystal growth and solidification experiments carried out under microgravity conditions." *Crystal Research and Technology*, 38(7–8), 726–733.

6. **Dagner, J., Friedrich, J., Müller, G.** (2007). "Time-dependent numerical simulation of the VGF process with a rotating magnetic field." *Journal of Crystal Growth*, 303(1), 245–249.

*Note on scope and gaps:* The core software-development and methodology papers for CrysVUn/CrysVUN++ are concentrated in the period 1999–2007, authored primarily by the Fraunhofer IISB Crystal Growth Laboratory group (Erlangen) — M. Kurz, G. Müller, J. Friedrich, and collaborators. Several closely related sources were identified but are **excluded** here because they are not peer-reviewed journal articles:

- M. Kurz, *Development of CrysVUN++, a software system for numerical modelling and control of industrial crystal growth processes*, PhD thesis, Univ. Erlangen-Nürnberg, 1998 (dissertation, not a journal article).
- Kurz, Pusztai, Müller, "CrysVUN++, a Powerful Computer Code for Global Thermal Modelling of Industrial Crystal Growth Processes," in *High Performance Scientific and Engineering Computing*, Lecture Notes in Computational Science and Engineering vol. 8, Springer, 1999 (book chapter/conference proceedings, not a journal).
- Various *Scientific.Net* / Materials Science Forum conference papers (e.g., on the MSL Thermal Modeling Tool) — trade/conference proceedings, not peer-reviewed journals in the strict sense.
- Friedrich, J. et al., "Erlangen—An Important Center of Crystal Growth and Epitaxy..." *Crystal Research and Technology* (2020) — a historical review that discusses CrysVUn but is not itself a paper describing/validating the code.

After 2007, CrysVUn was progressively merged with STHAMAS into the successor package **CrysMAS**, and I found no further standalone peer-reviewed journal articles specifically describing CrysVUn itself past this point — later papers using the tool (e.g., sapphire Kyropoulos growth studies) generally cite it via these earlier references rather than presenting new methodology for the code.
