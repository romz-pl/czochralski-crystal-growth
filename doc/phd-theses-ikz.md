# PhD Theses at the Leibniz-Institut für Kristallzüchtung (IKZ), Berlin

## Important note on completeness

The IKZ does not publish a single, centralized, machine-readable archive of all doctoral theses defended by its members. Doctoral degrees awarded to IKZ researchers are formally conferred by partner universities (chiefly TU Berlin, Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin, and occasionally other German or European universities), with the research work carried out at IKZ. Consequently, this list has been assembled from:

- IKZ's own "Section Fundamental Description" / "Model Experiments" group pages and staff publication lists
- University repositories (TU Berlin DepositOnce, etc.)
- Supervisor CVs/lecturing pages at IKZ (e.g., Prof. Matthias Bickermann's supervision list)
- IKZ Annual Reports and DGKK (German Association for Crystal Growth) newsletters
- Citations in recent IKZ-authored journal articles and preprints

**This list should be treated as a well-sourced but not guaranteed-exhaustive compilation.** IKZ's annual reports indicate several doctoral theses are completed each year (e.g., 5 defended in 2019 alone across all institute sections, only a fraction of which concern modeling/simulation specifically), and not all are indexed with full bibliographic detail in openly accessible sources. Entries below are restricted to theses whose subject matter is modeling, numerical simulation, or theoretical/computational analysis of crystal growth processes (as opposed to experimental growth, characterization, or materials-only theses).

## Why this list is short relative to IKZ's overall thesis output

IKZ's annual reports show a steady stream of completed doctorates (roughly 3–7 per year across the whole institute), but the overwhelming majority concern **experimental crystal growth, materials characterization, defect analysis, epitaxy, or device physics** rather than modeling/simulation specifically. The modeling/simulation-focused doctoral work is concentrated in two IKZ groups:

- **Section "Fundamental Description"** (numerical simulation of bulk crystal growth, KMC atomistic simulation, optimization and control) — led by Dr. Wolfram Miller / Natasha Dropka and colleagues
- **"Model Experiments" junior research group** (ERC-funded, multiphysics simulation validated by scaled model experiments) — led by Dr. Kaspars Dadzis

Given the difficulty of retrieving a complete official register through public web search, **I recommend independently verifying this list against**:

1. TU Berlin DepositOnce repository (depositonce.tu-berlin.de) — search "Kristallzüchtung," "Czochralski," "crystal growth simulation"

4. DGKK (Deutsche Gesellschaft für Kristallwachstum und Kristallzüchtung) Mitteilungsblätter, which regularly announce IKZ dissertations and the annual DGKK Young Scientist Award (a recurring recipient pool for IKZ-authored crystal growth theses, including modeling-focused ones)
5. Prof. Matthias Bickermann's supervision/committee page (ikz-berlin.de/~bickermann/e-students.html), which lists theses he has supervised or served on committees for — useful for cross-checking names, though it covers all IKZ thesis topics, not only modeling/simulation
