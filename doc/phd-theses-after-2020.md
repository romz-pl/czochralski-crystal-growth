## Confirmed PhD theses (verified bibliographic details)

### 1. Validation of Multiphysical Models for Czochralski Crystal Growth
- **Author:** Arved Wintzer
- **Institution:** Technische Universität Berlin
- **Year:** 2024
- **DOI:** [10.14279/depositonce-20957](https://doi.org/10.14279/depositonce-20957)
- **Topic:** Development and experimental validation of coupled multiphysical (heat transfer,
  electromagnetics, melt/gas flow, stress) numerical models for Czochralski (CZ) crystal growth,
  conducted within the ERC-funded NEMOCRYS project at the Leibniz Institute for Crystal Growth (IKZ)
  and IKZ's open-source simulation framework (`opencgs`, Elmer FEM).

### 2. Modeling the High-Pressure Optical Floating Zone Crystal Growth System and the High-Pressure, High-Temperature Growth of Single-Crystal Diamond
- **Author:** Sione S. Dossa
- **Institution:** University of Minnesota, Department of Chemical Engineering and Materials Science
- **Year:** 2022
- **Advisor:** Jeffrey J. Derby
- **Topic:** Numerical modeling of the optical floating-zone technique in a high-pressure, single-lamp
  furnace, and of high-pressure/high-temperature (HPHT) synthesis of single-crystal diamond; builds on
  global heat-transfer and melt-flow simulation approaches used throughout the Derby group's crystal
  growth modeling research.

## Recommended next steps for a complete bibliography

1. Search **ProQuest Dissertations & Theses Global** for "crystal growth" AND ("simulation" OR
   "modeling") with a 2021–2026 date filter.
2. Search **DART-Europe** and **EThOS** for the same terms to cover European and UK theses not in
   ProQuest.
3. Check institutional repositories directly for groups most active in this field: IKZ Berlin /
   TU Berlin, Fraunhofer IISB / Erlangen, TU Bergakademie Freiberg, University of Minnesota
   (Derby group), Montanuniversität Leoben (SiC/CD Laboratory).
4. Search Google Scholar for "PhD thesis" combined with the specific growth technique of interest
   (Czochralski, Bridgman, PVT/sublimation, floating zone, LPE, solution growth) to catch theses that
   cite themselves in later journal articles.
